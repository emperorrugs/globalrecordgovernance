## Phased Adoption

A typical national deployment follows three stages:

### Stage 1: Foundational
GRGF is introduced as an independent record layer attached to selected high-risk governance processes (e.g., procurement, licensing, approvals).

**Duration:** 3–6 months  
**Scope:** 1–3 government departments  
**Focus:** Proof of concept, stakeholder confidence

### Stage 2: Expansion
Additional institutions and sectors begin recording governance events under a shared authority framework.

**Duration:** 6–12 months  
**Scope:** 5–15 departments across multiple sectors  
**Focus:** Cross-institutional verification, policy harmonization

### Stage 3: Federation
GRGF operates as a national governance memory layer, supporting audits, courts, and oversight bodies. Cross-jurisdiction federation enables international cooperation.

**Duration:** 12–24 months  
**Scope:** National coverage with international federation  
**Focus:** Institutional memory, democratic accountability

## Key Principle

**At no stage does GRGF replace existing systems.**

It operates alongside registries, workflows, and decision tools, adding a verifiable governance layer without disrupting operations.

## Low-Capacity Deployment

In environments with limited technical infrastructure, GRGF can be deployed with:

- Minimal technical integration
- Manual or semi-automated event capture
- Focus on high-impact governance risks
- Progressive digitization as capacity grows

## 90-Day Pilot Framework

| Phase | Duration | Activities |
|-------|----------|------------|
| Setup | Days 1–30 | Environment provisioning, stakeholder alignment, scope confirmation |
| Evaluation | Days 31–60 | Policy encoding, deterministic testing, hash verification |
| Findings | Days 61–90 | Audit reconstruction, findings report, next-phase recommendations |
