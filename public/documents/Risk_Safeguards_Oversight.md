## Architectural Safeguards

**Safeguards are architectural, not procedural.** They cannot be bypassed by policy changes, administrative decisions, or political pressure.

### Immutability
- Records are append-only
- No deletion capability exists in the system
- No central override mechanism

### Verification
- Any authorized party can independently verify records
- Verification does not require privileged access
- Both existence and non-existence can be proven

### Federation
- No single node controls the network
- Cross-witnessed checkpoints prevent split-view attacks
- Each jurisdiction maintains sovereignty

## Transparency Governance

Transparency is governed by:

- **Legal context** — applicable laws and regulations
- **Privacy obligations** — data protection requirements
- **Institutional role** — access based on legitimate need

GRGF™ supports:
- Public verification without disclosure of sensitive content
- Confidential records with public existence proofs
- Graduated access levels based on institutional authorization
