## General Questions

### Is this a live system?
No. This website and its simulation are reference and demonstration tools only. No live governance records are created, stored, or modified.

### Does it store records?
No. The website does not store any governance records. Authoritative records exist only in sealed governance archives maintained outside this platform.

### Can governments use it?
Yes. Governments and institutions may reference GRGF as a governance standard, align policies to its principles, and use the simulation for training and review.

### Is it auditable?
Yes, by design. The framework is built to support independent audit and verification at every level.

### Is GRGF a blockchain?
No. GRGF uses cryptographic integrity techniques (hash chains, Merkle trees) but is not a blockchain. It does not use distributed consensus, cryptocurrency, or proof-of-work/stake mechanisms.

## Technical Questions

### How does verification work without exposing sensitive data?
GRGF uses cryptographic commitments (hashes) to prove record existence and integrity without revealing record content. Sensitive data remains encrypted; only existence proofs are publicly verifiable.

### What happens if a record is wrong?
Records are never deleted or modified. If an error is discovered, a superseding record is created that references the original, preserving the complete correction history.

### How does federation work?
Each jurisdiction operates its own sovereign GRGF node. Nodes cross-witness each other's checkpoints (Merkle roots) to prevent split-view attacks, without sharing sensitive data.

## Governance Questions

### Who controls GRGF?
GRGF is designed for neutral, independent governance. No single institution, vendor, or government has override authority. Anti-capture mechanisms (AC-01 through AC-05) prevent institutional capture.

### Does GRGF enforce laws?
No. GRGF records governance events — it does not interpret, enforce, or adjudicate. Enforcement authority remains with human institutions and legal systems.

### How is sovereignty preserved?
Each participating jurisdiction maintains full control over its own deployment, data, and policies. Federation is voluntary and does not create hierarchical authority.

## Adoption Questions

### How long does deployment take?
A controlled pilot can be operational within 90 days. Full national deployment follows a phased roadmap spanning 12–24 months.

### What does it cost?
Cost scales with deployment scope. GRGF's modular architecture allows incremental adoption, starting with high-impact governance processes.

### Is training provided?
Yes. GRGF includes a comprehensive capacity-building framework with role-based training modules for ministry staff, auditors, registry operators, and IT teams.
